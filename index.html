About the Titled Cup Format

The Title Cup is a year-long leaderboard competition for blitz. Players' best Titled Tuesday scores count toward the leaderboard, with prize money and qualification spots for Speed Chess Championship events on the line. Read the full overview
Players' best 20 Titled Tuesday scores count toward the Titled Cup Leaderboards. Players can compete in five different categories:

Open: all players.
Women: players who identify as women.
Juniors: players born in 2006 or later.
Girls: women players born in 2006 or later.
Seniors: players born in 1974 or earlier.

Players will be ranked based on the sum of their best 20 TT scores, counting only events in 2024.
Titled Tuesday (TT) is Chess.com's weekly event for titled players. The format is an 11-round Swiss in the 3+1 time control. There are two Titled Tuesday events every week, starting at 11 a.m. ET and 5 p.m. ET. Each event features a $2,500 prize fund. Click here for more details.
Players can qualify for Speed Chess Championship events based on their best 10 Titled Tuesday scores until the specified cutoff dates below:

Junior Speed Chess Championship: June 11
Speed Chess Championship: June 16
Women's Speed Chess Championship: August 6
Any titled player can join a Titled Tuesday.
You can keep up with all the chess action live on this site, or on participating streamers twitch channels!

Full event overview

The Titled Cup is a year-long leaderboard competition for blitz. Players' top performances in Titled Tuesday count toward the leaderboard, with players receiving prizes and qualifying for the Speed Chess Championship based on Titled Cup standings. The competition counts with a $100,000 prize fund and starts with the first Titled Tuesday of 2024 on January 2.

Players Schedule
 Format
 Prizes

Players

All players who participate in any Titled Tuesday tournament compete in the Titled Cup leaderboard.

Schedule

Players can start earning Titled Cup points as soon as the first Titled Tuesday of the year, on January 2. The competition ends on the last Tuesday of the year, December 31.

Format

Titled Cup Leaderboard

Players are ranked based on their 20 best Titled Tuesday scores (2024 events only). In addition to the overall results, players compete in four categories:

  • Juniors: players born in 2006 or later.
  • Girls: women players born in 2006 or later.
  • Women: players who identify as women.
  • Seniors: players born in 1974 or earlier.

Speed Chess Championship Qualification Leaderboards

Players can qualify for Speed Chess Championship events based on their Titled Cup performance. Players are ranked based on the sum of their best 10 Titled Tuesday scores (2024 events only) until the specified cutoff dates below:

  • Junior Speed Chess Championship: June 11
  • Speed Chess Championship: June 16
  • Women's Speed Chess Championship: August 6

Prizes

The Titled Cup features a $100,000 prize fund and awards players with spots in different Speed Chess Championship events.
