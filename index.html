Full event overview

The 2023 Champions Chess Tour Finals (CCT Finals) is the closing event of the Champions Chess Tour, Chess.com's most important event to date. GMs Magnus Carlsen, Hikaru Nakamura, Fabiano Caruana, and other stars will meet in Toronto, Canada, in a thrilling last clash for the title. The Finals start on December 9 at 12 p.m. ET/18:00 CET/22:30 IST and feature a $500,000 prize fund.

Format

The CCT Finals consist of a round-robin stage, a Survival stage, and a knockout. All games are in the 15+3 time control, except for tiebreak games.

Round-Robin Stage

  • Players compete in a round-robin where players contest two-game matches on every encounter.
  • Players are ranked based on the number of matches they win (click here for tiebreak information).
  • Players ranked first and second in the round-robin advance to the Semifinals.
  • Players ranked third through sixth go to the Survival stage.
  • Players ranked seventh and eighth get eliminated.

Survival Stage

  • Players ranked third through sixth compete in a double-elimination knockout.
  • Players ranked third and fourth start in the Winners Bracket, while players ranked fifth and sixth start in the Losers Bracket.
  • The winners of the Winners Finals and Losers Finals advance to the CCT Semifinals.

Knockout Stage

  • Players contest a four-player single-elimination bracket.
  • The first and second seeds are the players who ranked first and second in the round-robin stage, respectively. 
  • The top seed selects his opponent from among the two players who advanced from the Survival stage. 
  • Matches are in the best-of-three "set" format.
  • Each set consists of four games, with a bidding armageddon with a base time of 15 minutes as the tiebreaker.
  • If a third set is needed, it will consist of two games.
